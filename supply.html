Working Uniform

The Navy Working Uniform Type III is worn with an eight-pointed cover, a coyote brown t-shirt, a blouse, a pair of trousers, a belt, a buckle, a pair of black socks, and a pair of black boots. A name tape is worn over the right breast pocket, and a USNSCC/USNLCC branch tape is worn over the left breast pocket. Shoulder flashes indicate the wearerâ€™s association with the NSCC/NLCC. NWU Type III is the standard daily working uniform.

Specific regulations regarding this uniform can be found in Section 3704 of the Sea Cadets Uniform Manual.

Number issued per cadet: 2 sets

Dress Blues

The E1-E6 Service Dress Blue Uniform is worn with a white hat, a white t-shirt, a jumper, a pair of trousers, a neckerchief, a pair of black socks, and a pair of black service shoes. A name plate is worn over the right breast pocket, and a ribbon rack (where applicable) is worn on the left side. Shoulder flashes indicate the wearer's association with the NSCC/NLCC. A rating badge is worn on the right sleeve. A full dress uniform replaces the ribbon rack with medals and name plate with a six-ribbon-maximum ribbon rack. Dress Blues are worn for ceremonies, business occassions, and in formal and informal situations.

Specific regulations regarding this uniform can be found in Section 3702 of the Sea Cadets Uniform Manual.

Number issued per cadet: 1 set

Dress Whites

The E1-E6 Service Dress White Uniform is worn in the same fashion as Dress Blues. A belt and buckle is required for the trousers. The full dress uniform is worn with white gloves. The uniform is worn between the summer months of May and September.

Specific regulations regarding this uniform can be found in Section 3703 of the Sea Cadets Uniform Manual.

Number issued per cadet: 1 set

LC Winter Dress

The LC1-LC7 Winter Dress Uniform is composed of a black shirt, black trousers, white hat, black shoes, black socks, white undershirt, belt and buckle, and tie or necktab. Like the E1-E6 dress uniform, a name plate, ribbon rack, and rating badge are also worn.

Specific regulations regarding this uniform can be found in Section 3803 of the Sea Cadets Uniform Manual.

Number issued per cadet: 1 set

LC Summer Dress

The LC1-LC7 Summer Dress Uniform is the Winter Dress Uniform's summer counterpart. A white shirt replaces the black shirt, and no tie is part of the male uniform.

Specific regulations regarding this uniform can be found in Section 3802 of the Sea Cadets Uniform Manual.

Number issued per cadet: 1 set

Physical Training

The Physical Training Uniform is composed of a gold t-shirt, a pair of blue shorts, a pair of white socks, and a pair of athletic shoes. A sweater and sweats can be worn in cold weather. The uniform is worn for physical training or during after-hours in barracks.

Specific regulations regarding this uniform can be found in Section 6206 of the Sea Cadets Uniform Manual.

Number issued per cadet: 2 sets
